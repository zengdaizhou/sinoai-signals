## The Big Story

Tesla’s Full Self-Driving (FSD) technology is poised to enter China, and at least one major Chinese automaker is openly welcoming the move. XPeng’s vice president recently stated that the company might be the sole Chinese automaker to embrace Tesla’s FSD entry, citing shared reliance on pure vision-based autonomous driving systems. This development could validate XPeng’s technological approach while also allowing it to offer competitive localized solutions. Tesla’s FSD expansion into China marks a significant moment for the autonomous driving sector, potentially accelerating innovation and competition in the region. This comes as Tesla has already listed China in its FSD Supervised global availability map, signaling its readiness to deploy the technology in one of the world’s largest automotive markets.

## Signals

### Tencent Tests Project-Based Teams 
**What happened:** Tencent’s WeChat Group is piloting a shift from hierarchical management structures to project-based teams, aiming to streamline operations and empower younger talent. 
**Why it matters:** This move reflects broader efforts to rejuvenate leadership and accelerate AI innovation amid slowing growth.

### TSMC Employees Threaten Strike 
**What happened:** TSMC employees have protested alleged bonus cuts despite a 58% year-over-year net profit surge in Q1 2024, with some comparing the situation to Samsung’s labor disputes. 
**Why it matters:** The backlash highlights tensions between workforce expectations and corporate profitability, even in an era of record earnings.

### DeepSeek’s AI Platform Crashes Again 
**What happened:** DeepSeek’s AI service suffered its third major outage within a month, sparking user frustration and trending on social media. 
**Why it matters:** Repeated disruptions raise concerns about the platform’s stability and reliability, potentially impacting user trust.

### Tech Giants Compete for Grocery Platform 
**What happened:** Alibaba, JD.com, and Meituan are locked in a bidding war to acquire Pupuniao, a grocery delivery platform valued at $2–5 billion. 
**Why it matters:** The competition signals intensifying rivalry in the fresh e-commerce sector, with major players vying for dominance.
