Today: China's AI industry hits inflection points in monetization, hardware, and regulation.

The Big Story

China's AI industry faces a "Midway moment" where supply chains and monetization efficiency are becoming decisive in the global race. While Chinese models like DeepSeek V4 drive over 50% of global AI model calls, revenue remains concentrated among U.S. firms, with Anthropic capturing 31.4% share at $16.2 average revenue per user. This disconnect highlights a critical challenge: Chinese firms dominate usage but lag in profitability.

Major players are adopting divergent strategies—Tencent integrates AI into core services like ads and gaming, Alibaba bets on full-stack self-reliance from chips to apps, and ByteDance prioritizes user growth over monetization. The battle now hinges on sustaining AI's high marginal costs, with infrastructure like Tencent's data moats and Alibaba's cloud investments becoming key differentiators.

Signals

Huawei's 'Tao Law' Chip Breakthrough

What happened: Huawei unveiled a semiconductor design principle called "Tao Law" that reduces signal delay through "logic folding," with plans to achieve 1.4nm-equivalent transistor density by 2031.

Why it matters: This could help China circumvent advanced process restrictions and boost performance of chips like the Kirin 2026, expected in the Mate 90.

DeepSeek Tops Global AI Usage

What happened: DeepSeek-V4-Flash became the most-called AI model globally, with China's weekly usage hitting 9.22 trillion tokens—nearly double the U.S.

Why it matters: China's rapid AI adoption signals ecosystem maturity, but monetization remains a hurdle.

MIIT Pushes Autonomous Driving Standards

What happened: China's MIIT outlined 2026 priorities, including mandatory national standards for autonomous driving systems and simulation testing.

Why it matters: This accelerates China's bid to lead in smart vehicle regulations and safety frameworks.

Humanoid Robot Breakthrough

What happened: Robotera's Era0 model won RoboChallenge by mastering real-world tasks like sandwich-making and table-wiping.

Why it matters: It demonstrates China's progress in embodied AI and generalized physical operations.

AI Valuation Surge

What happened: Moonshot AI hit a $20 billion valuation, while DeepSeek eyes $350 billion, reflecting a shift toward capital dominance.

Why it matters: Chinese firms now rival global leaders in performance while operating at 98% lower costs.
