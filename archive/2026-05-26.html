Today: China's AI landscape shifts from user growth to monetization and hardware breakthroughs, while global rankings reveal stark revenue divides.

The Big Story

The latest Q1 2026 global LLM revenue rankings highlight a growing monetization gap between Western and Chinese AI firms. Anthropic leads with $134M in revenue (31.4% share) and a high $16.2 average revenue per user, while OpenAI’s 900M users generate just $2.2 ARPU. Meanwhile, ByteDance’s Doubao dominates China with 345M monthly active users but fails to appear on the revenue chart entirely. Tencent emerges as China’s top AI earner by embedding AI into ads and cloud services, yielding $2.9 ARPU from 114M users. The data underscores China’s challenge in converting massive user bases into sustainable AI revenue streams, with firms like Alibaba absorbing heavy R&D costs (84% profit drop) to pursue full-stack self-reliance.
