The Big Story

AI chip power consumption has reached a critical tipping point, with even leading GPUs now consuming over 1000W per chip. A breakthrough called Vertical Power Delivery (VPD) promises to redefine chip design by cutting resistance by 89% and slashing energy waste from 30% to under 5%. This revolution comes as AI infrastructure costs become unsustainable—combining hardware limitations with China's soaring model valuations creates immense pressure for efficiency breakthroughs that could reshape the global AI race.

The implications extend far beyond chip design. With power constraints limiting data center scalability and geopolitical tensions complicating supply chains, this power efficiency leap could determine whether new AI innovations remain viable or hit physical limits. China's push for localization in AI infrastructure makes these developments doubly strategic—if VPD delivers on its promise, it could accelerate self-sufficiency in high-performance computing.
