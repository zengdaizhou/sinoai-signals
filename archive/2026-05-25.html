The Big Story

DeepSeek's dramatic API price cuts signal a strategic shift in China's AI landscape, challenging global competitors like OpenAI and Google. By slashing costs to just 25% of previous levels, the company is prioritizing accessibility over short-term profits—a move reminiscent of disruptive tech plays like Pinduoduo. This aggressive pricing could reshape the economics of AI development, particularly as Western firms face budget overruns from soaring token costs. The gambit also highlights China’s growing influence in open-source AI, where it now leads in global downloads.
