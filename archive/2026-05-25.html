The Big Story

Tianjin is quietly emerging as a global powerhouse in robotics, defying expectations by focusing on practical industrial applications rather than flashy consumer-facing robots. While cities like Beijing and Shenzhen chase humanoid robots, Tianjin's cluster of over 200 robotics firms—including niche leaders in underwater drones and heavy-load AGVs—is solving real-world challenges in shipbuilding, logistics, and hazardous environments. The city's industrial robots already dominate 75% of its robotics output, well above the national average, with projections to exceed ¥25 billion by 2025.

This success stems from Tianjin's strategic leverage of its heavy-industry legacy, turning shipyards and factories into testing grounds for automation. Companies here avoid hype cycles by targeting B2B applications with measurable ROI, like Langyu Robotics' world-record 600-ton AGVs or Sublue's 65% global share in underwater drones. The model offers a blueprint for how industrial cities can reinvent themselves through specialization and infrastructure-driven innovation.
